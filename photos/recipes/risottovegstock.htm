Vegetable Stock
adapted from Ursula Ferrigno, 'Risotto, 30 simply delicious vegetarian recipes from an Italian kitchen,' Ryland Peters & Small, London 2001, ISBN 1 84172 147 6

PREP: 1½ hours.; Cooking time: 4¼ hours
Makes 4 to 5 cups

Homemade stock makes all the difference in flavor. You can make stock from whatever fresh vegetables are available, then refrigerate or freeze until needed.

  4   tablespoons olive oil (optional: use 2 tablespoons unsalted butter and 2 tablespoons olive oil)
  10   garlic cloves, crushed
  1   large onion, coarsely chopped
  4   leeks, washed and coarsely chopped
  2   carrots, coarsely chopped
  2   celery stalks, coarsely chopped
  1   fennel bulb, coarsely chopped
  1   handful of fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped
  2   dried bay leaves
  2   sprigs thyme (or ½ tsp dried flakes)

1.   Heat the oil in a large, heavy-bottomed saucepan. Add the garlic, saute for 2 minutes, then add the remaining ingredients, a little at a time, stirring constantly and cooking until softened but not browned.
 
2.   Add 2½ quarts of water and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for 1½ hours. Let cool 1¾ hours then strain the stock. Discard the solids.
 
3.   Return the the stock to the pan and bring to a simmer; simmer for 15 minutes. Boil rapidly until reduced by half, then use as needed.
