Greg's Onion Risotto
Gregory Swanson

PREP: 10 min; Cooking time: 18 to 20 min
Serves 4

It's hard to find a decent substitute for home-made stock, because ready-made stock usually lacks so much flavor. This recipe tries to compensate by including other ingredients that have strong flavors.

  3   14 1/2 ounce cans chicken broth (5 cups; e.g. Safeway, and not the low fat/low sodium)
  4   tablespoons butter
  1   tablespoon olive oil
  1   medium onion, finely chopped (about 1 cup)
  2   cups risotto rice (e.g. Carnaroli, packaged by Pila Vecia or Riseria di Lenta)
  1-1/2   cups white wine, e.g. Gallo Twin Valley Chardonnay
  4   cloves garlic, chopped
  1   cup parmasean cheese, grated

1.   Heat the stock until almost boiling. Keep it simmering.
 
2.   Heat the butter and oil in your favorite risotto pan over medium heat, then add the onions and garlic and cook until soft but not brown - about 2 minutes.
 
3.   Add rice and stir with wooden spoon until grains are coated - about 3 minutes. Pour in wine and stir until absorbed.
 
4.   Add 1 ladle of hot stock and simmer, stirring until it has been absorbed (you don't have to stir constantly - the mixture should bubble, don't let it cool to the point it stops bubbling). Continue to add the stock at intervals and cook as before, until the liquid has been absorbed and the rice is tender but still firm, about 18-20 minutes. Reserve the last ladle of stock.
 
5.   Remove from the heat, cover, and let rest for 2 minutes.
 
6.   Add the reserved stock, stir in the Parmesan cheese and mix well. Spoon into warmed bowls and serve immediately.
