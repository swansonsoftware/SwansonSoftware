Stuffed Bell Peppers
Gregory Swanson

PREP:15 min; Cooking time: 50 min


The original recipe called for brown rice instead of potatoes. I've never tried making this with brown rice because the potato version is so good.

  2   tbsps olive oil
  2   red bell peppers (you can substitute yellow or orange)
  ¼   medium onion, chopped
  4   cloves garlic, minced
  ½   tsp ground black pepper
  ½   tsp ground cumin
  ¼   tsp red pepper flakes
  ¼   16 oz jar chunkey salsa
  2   cups fresh baby spinach, chopped
  2   russet potatoes, peeled
  4   slices American cheese
  1   avocado, pitted and sliced

1.   Preheat oven to 375 degrees F.
 
2.   Cook the potatoes in boiling water for 20 minutes or until soft.
 
3.   As potatoes are cooking, slice the bell peppers in half. Remove seeds and cut away the white ribs on inside.
 
4.   Put the peppers on a 9x13 pan, drizzle olive oil on top. Cover with foil and bake 15 to 20 minutes so they start to soften.
 
5.   While peppers are baking, heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a pan and saute the onion, garlic, cumin, black pepper, and red pepper flakes. After about 3 minutes, add the salsa and spinach, mix and cook another 5 minutes.
 
6.   Add the potatoes to the pan and mash. Mix well, then remove the pan from the heat.
 
7.   Remove the peppers from the oven. Fill each pepper with the mixture. Cover and bake another 20-25 minutes.
 
8.   Remove foil and put cheese on top. Bake uncovered 7-10 minutes so cheese melts. Garnish with avocado.
