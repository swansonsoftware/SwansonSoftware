Risotto with Asparagus, Peas, and Basil
by Ursula Ferrigno, from 'risotto, 30 simply delicious vegetarian recipes from an Italian kitchen', Ryland Peters & Small, London 2001, ISBN 1 84172 147 6

PREP: 10 min; Cooking time: 18 to 20 min
Serves 4



  4   cups vegetable stock
  ¼   cup unsalted butter
  1   tablespoon olive oil
  8   shallots, finely chopped
  1½   cups risotto rice, such as vialone nano, carnaroli, or arborio
  ¼ - ½   cup white wine
  12   oz. asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch lengths
  1   cup fresh or frozen shelled peas
      finely grated zest of 1 lemon
  1½   cups freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  -   a large handful of fresh basil, leaves torn
  -   salt and freshly ground black pepper

1.   Heat the stock until almost boiling. Keep it simmering.
 
2.   Heat the butter and oil in your favorite risotto pan over medium heat, then add the shallots and cook until soft but not brown - about 2 minutes.
 
3.   Add rice and stir until grains are coated - about 1 minute. Pour in wine and stir until absorbed.
 
4.   Add 1 ladle of hot stock and simmer, stirring until it is absorbed. Repeat this procedure for 10 minutes, then add the asparagus, peas, and lemon zest, and mix well. Continue adding the stock as before, for 8 to 10 minutes, until the liquid has been absorbed. Reserve the last ladle of stock.
 
5.   Add the reserved stock, Parmesan, basil, salt, and pepper. Mix well. Remove from the heat, cover, and let rest for 2 minutes.
 
6.   Spoon into warmed bowls and top with basil and grated Parmesan.
