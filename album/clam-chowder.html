Clam Chowder

Gregory Swanson

PREP: 60 min; Cooking time: 2 hr 30 min

This is the New England (white) clam chowder. All chowders are made with pork, often bacon. I prefer to make this without any pork at all.

Stock

The stock can be made ahead of time and consider making a double batch; you can freeze half for use the next time. As the stock simmers the volume will reduce, concentrating the flavor. You can reduce it further, concentrating the flavor more.

Coarsley chopped leeks, piled high in a 6 quart pot.

Ingredients

3 lbs leeks, washed and coarsly chopped, including greens
6 cups water

Directions

  1. Put the water and chopped leeks in a large (6 quart) pot and bring to a boil
  2. Reduce heat to a simmer, and simmer uncovered for about 1 and 1/2 hours
  3. Push the leeks down into the liquid occasionally
  4. Strain and divide into 2 equal portions; each portion should be 2 cups. If you have more than 4 cups total, put the stock back in the pot and simmer until reduced to 4 cups

Chowder

Ingredients

3 lbs white potatos, peeled and chopped in 1/2-inch cubes
2 6.5 ounce cans chopped clams, save the juice (substitute 4 lbs. fresh clams)
2 cups stock from above
1 cup whipping cream
2 bay leaves
+ optional: 1/2 teaspoon salt
+ optional: 1 cup cooked bacon, drained and crumbled

Directions

  1. If using fresh clams: Rinse clams under cold water and put them in a steamer. Bring to a boil and steam the clams no more than four or five minutes. Rinse clams under cold water again. Save the steamer water, adjust it to 3 cups, and use it in step 3. Note: it is important to rinse the clams after steaming so they do not get overcooked and become tough. Continue to step 3.
  2. If not using fresh clams: Open cans of clams and strain juice into the stock. Combined stock and clam juice should be about 3 cups.
  3. Pour the combined liquid into a 2.5 quart pot and add the potatoes and bay leaves
  4. Bring to a simmer, and simmer partially covered 1 hour or until the liquid is almost all absorbed
  5. Let cool 15 minutes, then stir in the chopped clams
  6. Stir in the whipping cream; if the mixture is too thick after adding 1 cup of cream, add more until the desired thickness is reached
